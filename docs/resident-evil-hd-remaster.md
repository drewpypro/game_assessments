# Assessment: Resident Evil (HD Remaster)

**Type:** Game  
**Date:** 2025-12-01T00:00:00Z  
**Overall Score:** 5/5  

---

## Key Summary

Resident Evil HD Remaster is a modernized version of the classic survival horror game from Capcom, a highly reputable Japanese developer and publisher with a long history and strong franchise stewardship. The title does not use any known third‑party or kernel‑level anti‑cheat and is available DRM‑free on GOG, with only standard platform DRM on Steam, making it low‑intrusion from a security and privacy standpoint. Community sentiment on Steam is very positive, with around 90%+ positive reviews praising its faithful recreation of the original’s atmosphere and design, despite some dated mechanics and occasional PC performance quirks. Overall, it is a well‑regarded, low‑risk choice for players interested in classic survival horror.

## Dimensions

### Identity & Ownership (Score: 5/5)

Resident Evil HD Remaster is developed and published by CAPCOM Co., Ltd., a long‑established Japanese game company known for major franchises such as Resident Evil, Monster Hunter, and Street Fighter. Capcom has decades of history, strong brand recognition, and a consistent track record of supporting its flagship titles, so the developer and publisher are widely regarded as reputable and aligned with expectations for a high‑quality survival horror release.

**Developer:** CAPCOM Co., Ltd.  
**Publisher:** CAPCOM Co., Ltd.

---

### Anti-Cheat & Client Risk (Score: 4/5)

Resident Evil HD Remaster is a single‑player survival horror game with no competitive online component, and there is no indication on Steam, Capcom’s support pages, or PCGamingWiki-related resources that it uses any dedicated anti‑cheat system or invasive DRM such as kernel‑level drivers. The Steam version relies on the platform’s standard DRM, while the GOG release is explicitly advertised as fully DRM‑free. Overall, the game’s protections are minimal and non‑intrusive for typical PC use.

**Anti-cheat type:** none  
**Classification:** standard

---

### Community Sentiment (Score: 5/5)

Steam label: **Very Positive**  
Positive review %: **91%**

On Steam, Resident Evil HD Remaster holds a "Very Positive" overall user review label, with roughly 91% of more than ten thousand English reviews and a similar ratio across all languages being positive. Recent reviews also remain "Very Positive," indicating sustained goodwill over time. Players consistently praise the atmosphere, faithful modernization of the classic survival horror design, and visual upgrades, while common criticisms focus on dated mechanics, technical quirks on some PCs, and occasional performance issues that often require community fixes.
